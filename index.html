Complete Your Opinion Sentence

Choose ONE of these sentences. Complete it with YOUR thoughts. There are no wrong answers!

Option 1: My morning routine is _________ because _________. Examples: fast / slow, stressful / relaxing, boring / interesting, important
Option 2: I believe the best time of day is _________ because I usually _________. Examples: morning / afternoon / evening, exercise / relax / study
Option 3: The most important activity in my daily routine is _________ because _________. Examples: breakfast / school / exercise / family time, it keeps me healthy / I love it
Option 4: If I had more time, I would like to _________ instead of _________. Examples: sleep / exercise / read / spend time with family

üìù What You Will Do:

  • Choose ONE prompt that interests you most
  • Write your answer (2-3 words minimum per blank)
  • Read your answer to a partner (30 seconds)
  • Listen to your partner's answer
  • Ask a follow-up question like "Why?" or "Tell me more!"

Two Creative Scenarios

Your teacher will choose ONE scenario. Use your imagination!

Scenario A: Your Perfect Day

Imagine you have a perfect day with no school, no homework, and no responsibilities. What would your ideal daily routine be? Describe what you would do from morning to night.

What You Will Do:

  • Write 4-5 sentences describing your perfect day
  • Share your description with a partner
  • Listen to your partner's perfect day
  • Discuss: How is your perfect day different from your real routine?

Scenario B: Collaborative Storytelling

As a whole class, create an unusual or funny daily routine together! The teacher starts with one sentence, then each student adds ONE sentence to continue the story.

What You Will Do:

  • Listen to your teacher's opening sentence
  • Listen to each classmate's sentence
  • Contribute ONE silly sentence to the story
  • Laugh and enjoy the creativity together!

Daily Routines Around the World üåç

Did you know? Daily routines are very different in different countries!

  • Spain & Mexico: People have a long lunch break at 1-2 p.m., go home, rest, then work until 7-8 p.m.
  • United States & Canada: People have a short lunch break (30 minutes to 1 hour)
  • Japan: Students wear uniforms, have structured schedules, and stay at school for club activities after class
  • Italy & Greece: People eat their main meal at lunch time, not in the evening

Discuss These Questions

1. In some countries, people have a long lunch break. How is this different from your country? 2. Do you think a long lunch break is a good idea? Why or why not? 3. What is a typical school day like in YOUR country? 4. In your family or culture, do you have any special routines or traditions? 5. How has your daily routine changed in the last 5 years?

üìù What You Will Do:

  • Listen to your teacher explain different countries' routines
  • Discuss these questions with a partner or small group
  • Share about your own country's school day
  • Write 2-3 sentences about a special tradition in YOUR family
  • Learn how routines differ around the world!
